# Recruiting Pipeline & Hiring Capacity System

A system built on integrated recruiting funnel data to diagnose hiring process inefficiencies and support capacity planning and sourcing decisions — not just pipeline reporting.

## Problem

Recruiting teams often track activity volume without visibility into where the process actually breaks down. Conversion rates are reviewed in aggregate, time-to-hire is reported as a single number, and sourcing decisions are based on cost rather than downstream outcomes. Without stage-level and segment-level analysis, bottlenecks persist undetected and hiring plans are built on assumptions.

## System Logic

The system is built around four connected views of the recruiting funnel:

- **Stage conversion mapping** — pass-through rates at each pipeline stage by role, team, and recruiter, isolating where candidates are lost and whether drop-off is structural or inconsistent
- **Time-to-hire decomposition** — cycle time broken into stage-level durations, surfacing where delays concentrate rather than treating hiring speed as a single metric
- **Drop-off diagnostics** — identification of the stages and candidate segments with the highest attrition, distinguishing process friction from candidate quality issues
- **Source effectiveness** — sourcing channel performance measured not by application volume but by conversion quality, offer acceptance, and early-stage retention

## What This Enables

- Targeted process improvements at the stages that actually constrain hiring velocity
- Sourcing budget allocation based on quality-of-hire indicators, not just cost-per-applicant
- Capacity planning informed by realistic cycle times per role type and seniority
- Recruiter and hiring manager feedback grounded in funnel data rather than anecdotal experience
